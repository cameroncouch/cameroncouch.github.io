Service Shelf

Built using: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, JSON

After being baffled by the amount of items on screen while paying a bill online, I conceived this a way to combine all third-party widgets, such as chat, help, feedback, along with any other floating UI elements a website may have, such as a "scroll top" or shopping cart button.

The client can customize a JSON configuration file to control every aspect of the shelf element, like the amount of segments contained within it. The client would also add in the necessary callback functions to launch each respective widget.

This gives the user the ability to have a less inhibited browsing experience. This is further enabled by enabling the shelf to be draggable to different parts of the viewport.

Things to improve: Drag & drop responsiveness, fix bugs, refactor JS.

Utility for Transformers: The Trading Card Game

Built using: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, AngularJS, node.js, Express, and PostgreSQL DB

This was a personal project that I built as a skills test during my bootcamp experience, and I tied it into my nerdy obsession at the time, playing a trading card game!

The utility is used as replacement for pen + paper, tokens, or other means of tracking damage dealt through the course of a game of Transformers: The Trading Card Game. It also provides solutions to advanced game mechanics, such as combining cards into one "super-bot" that changes the health value, and the character that is in the playfield.

Users are also able to get up-to-date pricing on their collection by using the pricing tool, which is powered by the TCG Player REST API.

Things to improve: CSS, fix bugs, IndexedDB, refactor JS.

About Me

I am a passionate and resourceful person. Like many people I'm sure, I get very focused on something I'm excited about, but I've learned to temper that spark in order to stay engaged for the long term, and not lose sight of other things

Video Games

Video Games have been a huge part of my life since I can remember. Growing up playing hand-me-down Atari, Coleco, and NES, led to me consuming Game FAQs, monthly 'zines, and bugging my local FuncoLand during my daily treks home from school. I've shared this passion with my wife, and we play co-op and competitive games as a part of our lazy days in. I've also strengthened the relationship between my 3 brothers and I, as we play co-operative games like Sea of Thieves and Deep Rock Galactic as a way to catch up with one another.

One trend in gaming that I'm particularly touched by is the purposeful inclusion of accessibility options in games and game hardware. My dad had a stroke in the 2010's, and has recently been able to find games that allow him to experience the joy of gaming once again.

Career

My career started at 14 years old working part-time at a local Steakhouse, and part-time at GameStop (the old FuncoLand). I earned my associates and bachelors degree while also managing GameStop stores. Some of my most formative experiences were during this time. I learned that I loved connecting with people over Video Games, finding gaming solutions for them (which was especially important before the advent of YouTube), and eventually helping to shape the lives of people and their careers during my time as a leader there.

After giving GameStop a 3-month resignation notice, and saving money, I began driving 2 hours a day to Detroit to attend a Coding Bootcamp, where I would learn the basics of programming and how to build applications using JavaScript.

I currently work in the Digital Analytics space, where I lead a team of JavaScript developers in consulting and writing custom solutions for our custommer-base. We work with C-Suite and individual contributors for Fortune 100 - 500 companies to implement our product and develop solutions that meet their needs. As a leader, I find myself again helping to enrich the live's of others. I also have the autonomy to work cross-functionally improve the company, our people, and our customer's experience.
